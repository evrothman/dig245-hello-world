I worked through the "keyboard shortcuts & workflows" section and found that while some of the keyboard shortcuts were helpful, others either didn't work on windows or took me longer to execute that doing it how I normally would. As an example, control +tab simply changed the tab I was in rather than switching to a text editor. Similarly, after pressing windows + tab I was met with a page full of applications, and it likely have been faster to simply click the one I needed to open. However, some workflows were significantly more helpful, such as control +f and control tab. Overall, I think that it is useful to incorporate some keyboard shortcuts into my workflow, but I won't be abandoning my mouse just yet.
  6. Remarkable projects
    • March Through Time: Presented by Time in Fortnite
      This is a fascinating project that attempts to meet young audiences where they are, in this case, roaming the rpg/fps game that is fortnite. This collaboration allows players to listen go to an event in game that recounts the MKL Jr.'s historic I have a dream speech along with a museum and other elements. It is simply wild to see a video game create in person events meant to educate kids an important topic that is largely unrelated to itself.
    • The 1619 Project
      This initiative by the New York Times works to reframe the History of the United States around slavery and race. This project is important because it tells a perspective that is often left out of history books and consequently de-ephasized in the minds of young people.
    • The Wayback Machine
      The Wayback Machine is a digital archive for billions of web pages. It periodically saves the code of a website and time/date stamps it, then adds it to the archive so people can see how that website has changed over time. Its a fascinating projects that allows people to actually see the progress that the internet has made in terms of UX, UI, and accessibility.
